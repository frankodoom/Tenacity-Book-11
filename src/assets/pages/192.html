A Around the country there are weather stations where information is collected about
temperature, precipitation (rainfall), wind direction and speed and atmospheric pressure. In
addition, data is obtained from satellites in space and radar. All this data is sent on a daily
basis, or more frequently than that, to the National Meteorological Agency where it is analysed
using powerful computers. These apply physical laws to the data to create mathematical models
which are then used to produce charts, weather and air-quality forecasts, climate predictions,
risk assessments and early warning services.
