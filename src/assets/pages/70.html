Unit 3 Traditional and modern medicine
Now look at this sentence.
I can treat hiireenaa daga or anaemia by making a drink with fresh togoo leaves, which I boil
and mix with sugar.
The relative clause which I boil and mix with sugar is a non-defining relative clause. It does not
tell us which togoo leaves we are talking about – it is talking about any togoo leaves. The words
which I boil and mix with sugar add extra information.
Notes
1 Non-defining relative clauses are separated from the rest of the sentence by commas.
Compare these two sentences:
a
Atiga has two brothers who serve in the army.
Without commas, this sentence means that Atiga probably has more than two brothers but only
two of them serve in the army: it is defining the two brothers.
b
Atiga has two brothers, who serve in the army.
With commas, this sentence means that Atiga has only two brothers, both in the army: it is
adding information about the two brothers.
2 We cannot replace who or which with that in non-defining relative clauses.
