B2.5 Increase your word power: Prefixes
A prefix is a group of letters added to the beginning of a word which changes its meaning and
makes a new word.
Many prefixes give the opposite meaning to the word they are put in front of. They are;
un- , in-, dis- These can be added to many adjectives
Example:
unhappy, incomplete, dishonest
in- changes to im- before adjectives beginning with ‘m’ or ‘p’
Example:
impatient
in- changes to ir- before adjectives beginning with ‘r’
Example:
irresponsible
in- changes to il- before adjectives beginning with ‘l’
Example:
illegal
un- and dis- These can also be added to many verbs
Example:
unlock, disappear

anti- against e.g. antisocial

mid- middle e.g. midday
