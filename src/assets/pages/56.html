B2.6 Speaking: Pronunciation – Contrastive sentence stress
In every sentence, important information words are stressed. Sometimes, though, one word in a
sentence is given very strong stress.
Example: A: Can you 'play the gui'tar?
B: No, I can play the 'drums.
In the second sentence drums has very strong stress because it is new information and contrasts
with guitar. Play is also stressed, but weakly because it does not give any new information.
