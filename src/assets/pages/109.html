A4.7 Reading: Myths about HIV / AIDS
1
With a partner, look at these beliefs about HIV / Aids. Discuss which of them are myths.
a It is said that the drug treatment for HIV / Aids is so much worse than the disease itself that it’s
not worth it.
b It is believed by many that a woman who is HIV positive cannot conceive a child.
c Aids is said to be a death sentence.
d Most young people today are believed to have sex outside marriage and it is unrealistic to expect
them to stop.
e The HIV / Aids situation in Africa is commonly believed to be hopeless and out of control.
f It is a common belief that if you take a bath or shower after having sex, you reduce the risk of
HIV infection.
g It is said that everyone who is HIV positive gets Aids.
myth / mɪθ / noun [countable, uncountable]
1 an idea that many people believe, but which is not true: myth of the myth of
male superiority myth that the myth that the disease only affects older people
2 an ancient story that explains a natural or historical event
