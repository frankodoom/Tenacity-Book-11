Where should the new secondary school go?
The Ministry of Education has decided to open another secondary school in your region.
Members of the public have been asked to give their opinions about where the new school
should be located.
With a partner you must discuss some possible sites for the new secondary school and select one. You
must consider the advantages and disadvantages of different locations. Bear in mind the following:
• how close it is to an area of large population
• how close it is to main roads, main bus routes and if relevant, a railway station
• if there is space for a sports field.
When you have chosen your location, be prepared to tell the rest of the class about it.
Before you start:
• Prepare for the activity carefully, as you have for the other discussion activities in this unit. Make
a list of points to make.
• Look back at ways of expressing advantages and disadvantages in A1.8 and the language of
meetings in B1.5. Try to include some of this language in your contributions.
