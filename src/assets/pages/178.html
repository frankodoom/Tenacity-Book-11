A7.6 Increase your word power: Discourse markers
Discourse markers are words and expressions which show the relationship between different parts
of a discourse or text in order to present a logical arrangement of ideas.
Discourse markers are linking words and expressions which generally occur at the beginning of
a sentence.
They can have various functions, as shown in the table.
Function Example
adding information
In addition
contrasting a piece of information in relation to another
However
emphasising a piece of information in relation to another
Of course
sequencing information in a list
Firstly
introducing the cause or reason for something or effect of something
Due to the fact that
Introducing the effect or consequence of something
Consequently
introducing a new topic
With regard to
introducing a personal comment or opinion
In my view
introducing examples or illustrations of the previous piece of information
