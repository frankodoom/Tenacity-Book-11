A4.3 Language focus: Giving advice
We can use various modals to give people advice
a should
You should … You shouldn’t …
b ought to
You ought to …
These are both used in the same way to suggest something
that is a good idea. Ought to is not used as often as should,
and is rarely used in questions or in the negative.
You should make sure your younger brothers and sisters know
about HIV / AIDS.
You ought to make sure your younger brothers and sisters
know about HIV / AIDS.
c can
You can … You can’t …
We use this to suggest a possible course of action.
You can help people who are HIV positive by being their friend
and supporting them when they are ill.
d could
You could …
This is used in place of can when we want to give advice
politely or if we are not sure that something is possible.
If you want to help, you could volunteer at your local
HIV / AIDS centre.
e must
You must … You mustn’t …
We use this when we want to give advice that is urgent or
essential.
You must take your ART medicine regularly every day.
f Had better
You’d better … You’d better not …
This is also used to give a strong recommendation.
You’d better see a doctor immediately.
