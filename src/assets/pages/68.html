A3.6 Increase your word power: make and let
make
One meaning of make is to force someone to do something or cause something to happen
Examples:
• A policeman made me get off my bicycle as I was wobbling too much.
• The film made me cry it was so sad.
• My brother can make almost any broken car work.
• My mother doesn’t make me work in the garden; I do it to help her.
Note the structure:
Subject + MAKE + person or thing + infinitive without TO
let
The verb let can be used in this way, with the meaning of allow
• I’m letting my hair grow.
• The librarian lets us study in the library until 7 p.m.
• The librarian doesn’t let us bring food into the library.
The structure is the same as make:
Subject + LET + person or thing + infinitive without TO
