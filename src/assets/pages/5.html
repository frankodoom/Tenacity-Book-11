INTRODUCTION TO THE DIGITAL VERSION OF ENGLISH FOR ETHIOPIA

Welcome to the digital version of English for Ethiopia, Student Textbook Grade 11. This project was developed by Tenacity Worldwide, a team of educators dedicated to improving English teaching through technology. This new version has many exciting digital features:

  • Videos
  • Audio recordings
  • Interactive exercises
  • Dictionary links

You can access the book directly at the Tenacity Worldwide website, or your school will access it for you.


GETTING STARTED


In the upper left corner of English for Ethiopia, Student Textbook Grade 11, you will see the blue and white Menu Icon. When you click on this icon, the main menu will open with links to the Introduction, Authorsâ€™ Page, Table of Contents, Textbook units, and Appendixes.

There are twelve regular units and four revision units. One revision unit comes after every three regular units. There are four Appendix sections at the end of the book: Verb forms, Phrasal verbs, Vocabulary list, Listening texts, and Writing a formal letter and CV.

Use the Previous button and the Next button in the upper right corner of every page. Click on these buttons to go to the next page or back to the previous page. If you have a keyboard, you can also move forward and back by using the right and left arrows on your keyboard.

To return to the main menu, click on the Menu Icon on every page. If you are using a smartphone to look at this book, remember to turn the phone to a landscape/horizontal position. This will give you the best view and make the print most clear.


USING THE NEW FEATURES

Videos

The Ethiopian Ministry of Education has produced a series of videos to go with English for Ethiopia, Student Textbook for grades 9, 10, and 11. The videos cover the same topics and skills that are in a unit. They also add more exercises in the regular units of the textbooks. The videos are presented by native English speakers and include a person using sign language. You can use the videos with the rest of the units or use them separately for extra information and review. Your teacher will tell you what to do with the videos. If you are studying by yourself, you can decide how to use them. The videos in Student Textbook Grade 11 appear in a large black video screen in the units. To play a video, click on the Play arrow on the lower left part of the video screen. Click on Pause to stop the video. Click on the white line and circle below the Play button to move forward and back in the video.


Audio recordings

The digital version of English for Ethiopia has audio recordings for many kinds of listening activities. Reading passages are also recorded to help you understand what you read. You can listen while you read.

Every audio activity begins with instructions about what you are to do. To listen to an audio recording, click on Play on the lower left part of the Audio Player. Click on Pause to stop the recording. Click on the line to move back and forth, so you can listen to parts of the recording again.

For Dictation activities, listen to the audio recording of a passage, Pause the recording, and go back or forward as necessary, to write down everything you hear. Write on a piece of paper or in your exercise book. To check your answer, click on the blue Show Text button

For audio recordings of reading passages, listen as you read the text. Some long reading passages are on more than one page. Look for the Audio Player at the top of the next page or pages.


Interactive Exercises

There are five types of interactive exercises: Fill in the Blanks, Multiple Choice, Drag and Drop, True/False, and Dictation. When you type in words to answer Fill in the Blanks and Dictation exercises, it is important to use the exact same words you read or heard. The interactive exercises appear in two parts of the book:

  • There is a set of five or six exercises that review information you learned at the end of each regular unit (1-12). These exercises come after the Assessment section and are identified by the words: Interactive Exercises.
  • There are more interactive exercises in the four Revision Units (after Units 3, 6, 9, 12). These units have a combination of interactive and non-interactive exercises. You can do the interactive exercises right on the screen of your computer, cell phone or other device. When you finish an exercise you can check your answer by clicking the blue Check button . For the non-interactive exercises, you can write your answers or your essay on a separate piece of paper or in your exercise book.

For some exercises, you will see only the first question in a set of five or six questions. To go to the next question, click on the blue arrow at the bottom of each question.

Sometimes you will only see directions for an exercise, followed by a blue button that says Start Quiz . Click on the blue button to start the exercise. To check your answers after you do an interactive exercise, click on the blue Check button at the end of the exercise.

After you do a Dictation, click on the blue Show Text button to see the complete passage and compare what you wrote.


Dictionary links

Appendix 3 (at the end of the book) has a list of important vocabulary words from the twelve regular units. These words are listed alphabetically by unit. They are underlined in blue and linked to a digital dictionary. The same words appear in each unit of the textbook, underlined in blue. When you click on an underlined word, a window opens with a definition of the word. For many of the words, you can click on an audio recording to hear the pronunciation.

Good luck and have fun!
